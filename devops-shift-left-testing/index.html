Are you looking for ways to accelerate the development process and reduce costs? Shifting testing to the left should be on your radar. Automated testing is a practice that helps accelerate your flow of value to the market, and if you haven’t started building automated testing yet, you should start ASAP!

Shift-left testing is a practice of testing earlier in the development lifecycle, allowing teams to catch bugs before they become costly and time-consuming issues.

In this blog, I’ll explain what DevOps shift-left testing is, the benefits and challenges of the practice, and how to implement it. I’ll also provide tips for effective shift-left testing, best practices for automation, and advice on monitoring and analyzing this type of testing.

Introduction to DevOps Shift-Left Testing

DevOps shift-left testing is the practice of shifting testing activities to the left in the software development process. It involves testing at each stage of the software development lifecycle (SDLC) instead of waiting until the end. This shift-left approach enables teams to catch and address any issues much earlier in the process, saving time and money.

To give some context on the difference between shifting testing to the left and a more traditional testing process, think about a Waterfall project flow. In the waterfall, delivery steps are completed and moved down the assembly line to the next station to get validated or integrated. If there is an issue, it can bring the entire assembly line to a halt.

By moving testing activities earlier in the development cycle, aka shifting left on a delivery timeline, your quality engineering staff are proactively defining the conditions for success through automation. This turns the tables around where quality issues are discovered and remediated causing fewer impacting blockages in your pipelines.

By moving testing activities to the left, teams can find and fix bugs and other issues before they become costly and time-consuming problems. This is crucial for any organization that wants to succeed in the ever-changing world of technology. With DevOps shift-left testing, organizations can reduce the time and cost associated with testing, speed up the development process, and improve the overall quality of their products.

What is Shift-Left Testing?

Shift-left testing is the practice of testing earlier in the software development process and shifting activities to the left on a product delivery timeline. It involves testing at each stage of the SDLC, rather than waiting until the end. This shift-left approach enables teams to catch and address any issues much earlier in the process, saving time and money.

Organizations that do proactive planning and discovery on requirements, architecture, and design should also be conceptualizing their quality controls early and often.

Through years of continuous improvement, auto manufacturers have made huge strides to integrate test automation into every stage of their delivery processes. They have done this to reduce their time to market and increase their overall quality.

Shift-left testing helps technology organizations reduce the time and cost associated with testing, speed up the development process, and improve the overall quality of their products. It also allows teams to gain better visibility into their development process, identify potential issues before they become costly problems, and ensure that the quality of their product meets customer expectations.

Shifting Left with testing is also a core tenant of practicing Agile DevOps. Complex systems become easier to manage over the long term when automated testing is integrated early in the development lifecycle.

Benefits of Shift-Left Testing

Shift-left testing offers many benefits for organizations that are looking to accelerate the development process and reduce costs. Here are some of the key benefits of this practice:

  1. It reduces the time and cost associated with testing. By shifting testing activities to the left, teams can catch and address issues much earlier in the process, saving time and money.
  2. It speeds up the development process. With shift-left testing, teams can quickly identify and address any issues, reducing the amount of time it takes to develop a product.
  3. It improves the overall quality of the product. By testing earlier in the process, teams can ensure that the product meets customer expectations and has fewer bugs.
  4. It allows teams to gain better visibility into their development process. With shift-left testing, teams can identify potential issues before they become costly problems.
  5. It increases customer satisfaction. By testing earlier in the process and ensuring that the quality of the product meets customer expectations, organizations can improve customer satisfaction.

Challenges of Shift-Left Testing

Although shift-left testing offers many benefits, it also comes with certain challenges. Here are some of the key challenges of this practice:

  1. It requires a change in mindset. Organizations must shift their mindset from testing at the end of the development process to testing at each stage of the SDLC.
  2. It requires a shift in resource allocation. Organizations must allocate more resources to testing activities earlier in the process.
  3. It requires more collaboration between teams. Organizations must ensure that all teams involved in the development process are collaborating effectively to ensure that shift-left testing is effective.
  4. It requires a shift in tools and technologies. Organizations must ensure that they have the right tools and technologies in place to support shift-left testing.
  5. It requires more communication. Organizations must ensure that all stakeholders are kept informed of any changes or issues related to shift-left testing.

  2. Develop a plan. Develop a plan that outlines how you will implement shift-left testing, including which tools and technologies you will use and how you will measure success.
  3. Train your teams. Ensure that all teams involved in the development process are trained on the principles of shift-left testing and how to use the tools and technologies.
  4. Monitor progress. Monitor the progress of shift-left testing and identify any issues that need to be addressed.
  5. Adjust as needed. Adjust the plan as needed to ensure that the process of shift-left testing is effective and efficient.
